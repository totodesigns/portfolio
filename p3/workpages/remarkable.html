Product Shooting of reMarkable.

Photography

Concepting

In this workshop, I had the great honor of making one of Hokusai’s paintings move. The workshop taught me AfterEffects and the use of keyframes to create animations. Along with Photoshop to cut out pieces from the painting, I put them all together in one final animation by editing the short video in PremierePro.

My role.

I was alone in this work, and I thought out the concept, did the shooting, and edited the pictures.

Concept.

reMarkable markets their product by calling it “The Paper Tablet”. It’s a note-taking tablet, but the technology is so cutting-edge that it feels like you’re writing on paper. I wanted to enhance that concept by shooting pictures using that product slogan.

So my shooting concept was placing the reMarkable tablet on top of scattered pieces of paper everywhere, but I quickly came to realize that it was important to highlight the thickness of the tablet, and I wouldn’t be able to do that from a top-view.

I then decided to make it stand up. They also write “It’s paper, with magic powers.”, therefore I wanted some pieces of paper to stand up “magically”.

Shooting

Product picture without paper in the background.

I used tape and two colleagues to hold up each piece of paper. Now it looks like they are flying.

I placed the reMarkable leaned up against a heavy object and stuck it so it didn’t fall flat down.

Editing.

I used Photoshop first to remove the tape from the picture. Since the background was already kind of infinite, I didn’t fix much in Lightroom. I adjusted the shadings a bit, and that was it.

Edited product picture with paper in the background.

Reflections.

I’m happy with the concept, and I believe I have highlighted their product slogan by using their concept and transferring it to photography.

The output was a bit too simple for me. I had ideas during the shooting how I wanted it to turn out, and when I was editing, I realized that there wasn’t more to it.

I learned a lot about the exposure triangle and how it affects a picture. But for this specific shooting for the reMarkable, I didn’t apply that many changes within the triangle. But!

Fun fact: I used the same learnings to take portraits that you see on the front page.
